End Gun Violence

What is Gun Violence?

Gun Violence is violence commited with the use of a fire-arm. It may or may not be considered criminal. Criminal violence includes homicide, assault with deadly weapon, and attempted suicide or suicide.

What are the Effects of Gun Violence?

People who are impacted by gun violence may experience stress, depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). These effects also extend to witnesses, bystanders and the loved ones of victims. According to a 2018 study by the Pew Research Center, the majority of U.S. teens fear a shooting could happen at their school, with students of color express a higher level of concern than white students.

How Can We Reduce or Prevent It?

There is not a solution to stopping Gun Violence but there are ways in which we can reduce and prevent it globally:

•Implement Universal Background Checks.
•Prohibit Firearm Access for High-Risk Groups.
•Focusing on Guns and Gang Violence.
•Require Child Safety Features for Every Gun.
•Improving Police Practices, Accountability and Legitimacy.
•Ban Assault Weapons.
•Require Background Checks for All Sales.
•Firearm Safety Training.
